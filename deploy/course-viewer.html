Part 2: 5 Automations That Save 10+ Hours/Week

Automation 1: Instant Lead Follow-Up

Time saved: 2-3 hours/week

When a new lead fills out a form or calls, they get an immediate text + email within 60 seconds — even at 2 AM.

Setup:

  • Trigger: New form submission or missed call
  • Action 1: Send personalized SMS "Hey {name}, thanks for reaching out! We got your message and will be in touch within the hour."
  • Action 2: Send email with your info + booking link
  • Action 3: Notify your team in Slack/Discord

Tools needed: GoHighLevel (or Zapier + your CRM)

Automation 2: Review Request Sequence

Time saved: 1-2 hours/week

After completing a job or appointment, automatically request a Google review via SMS.

Setup:

  • Trigger: Job marked complete / appointment finished
  • Wait: 2 hours
  • Action: Send SMS "Hey {name}, thanks for choosing {business}! If you had a great experience, would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? {review_link}"
  • Wait: 3 days
  • Action: If no review, send follow-up

Tools needed: GoHighLevel or Make

Automation 3: Social Media Content Pipeline

Time saved: 3-4 hours/week

Generate a week of social media posts in 30 minutes using AI, then schedule them.

Setup:

  • Step 1: Ask ChatGPT "Create 7 social media posts for a [your business type] in [your city]. Each post should have a hook, value, and call-to-action."
  • Step 2: Copy the posts into Canva to create graphics
  • Step 3: Schedule all 7 at once using GoHighLevel social planner or Buffer

Tools needed: ChatGPT + Canva + scheduler

Automation 4: Appointment Reminders

Time saved: 1-2 hours/week

No-shows cost money. Automated reminders cut no-shows by 50%+.

Setup:

  • 24 hours before: SMS "Reminder: You have an appointment tomorrow at {time}. Reply C to confirm or R to reschedule."
  • 2 hours before: SMS "Your appointment is in 2 hours. See you at {address}!"
  • If no confirmation: Auto-alert your team

Tools needed: GoHighLevel (built-in) or any CRM with SMS

Automation 5: Invoice & Payment Reminders

Time saved: 1-2 hours/week

Stop chasing payments manually.

Setup:

  • When invoice is 3 days overdue: Send friendly reminder email
  • When 7 days overdue: Send SMS reminder
  • When 14 days overdue: Send final notice + alert your team
  • When paid: Send thank-you message

Tools needed: Make/Zapier + your invoicing tool (QuickBooks, Stripe, etc.)

Module 1: What Is AI (And Why Should You Care?)

Welcome

Lesson 1: AI in Plain English

What AI Actually Is

Forget the Hollywood robots. Forget the scary headlines. Here's what AI really is:

AI is software that does tasks that normally require a person.

That's it. It reads, it writes, it talks, it looks at pictures, it makes decisions. Not perfectly, but well enough to save you hours every single week.

Think of it like this:

  • 2005: You needed a bookkeeper to track your expenses. Then QuickBooks showed up and did most of it automatically.
  • 2015: You needed a receptionist to answer every call. Then automated phone systems could handle basic questions.
  • 2025: You needed a copywriter, a social media manager, a customer service rep, and a data analyst. Now AI can handle a big chunk of all four.

Every few years, software gets smarter and takes over another piece of work that used to require a full-time person. AI is just the latest — and biggest — jump in that pattern.

What AI Is NOT

MythReality
"AI will replace my business"AI replaces tasks, not businesses. It's a tool, like a power drill vs. a screwdriver.
"AI is too complicated for me"If you can send a text message, you can use AI. Most tools work just like chatting.
"AI is only for big companies"AI tools today cost $20-50/month. A local pizza shop can use the same tech as Amazon.
"AI will sound robotic and hurt my brand"Modern AI writes better than most people. You review everything before it goes out.
"I need to learn to code"Absolutely not. Every tool in this course works with plain English — just type what you want.

The Real Story

Here's what actually happened that made AI useful for regular businesses:

For decades, computers could only follow exact instructions. "If this happens, do that." They couldn't understand language, recognize patterns, or make judgment calls.

In the last few years, that changed. Computers got good enough at understanding language that you can now just tell them what you want in plain English and they figure it out.

That's the revolution. Not smarter computers — computers you can actually talk to.

Lesson 1: The Lead Response Machine

The fastest way to lose a customer? Make them wait.

Here's the reality:

Response TimeChance of Closing
Under 5 minutes9x more likely
Under 1 hour7x more likely
Under 24 hours5x more likely
Over 24 hoursAlmost zero

Most local businesses take 12-48 hours to respond to leads. By then, the customer has already called someone else.

The fix: An automation that responds instantly and keeps following up until they book or say no.

Building Your Lead Response Automation

In GoHighLevel:

Step 1: Create the workflow

  1. Go to Automation → Workflows → Create New
  2. Name it "New Lead Response"
  3. Set the trigger: "Contact tag added" → tag name: "new-lead"

Step 2: Instant response (0 minutes)

  1. Add action: Send SMS
  2. Message: "Hi {first_name}! Thanks for reaching out to [Business Name]. I got your message and I'll be in touch shortly. In the meantime, feel free to book a time that works for you here: [booking link]"
  3. Add action: Internal notification (email to you) — "New lead: {first_name} {last_name} — {phone} — {email}"

Step 3: Follow-up 1 (24 hours later, if no appointment booked)

  1. Add action: Wait → 24 hours
  2. Add condition: "Has appointment booked?" → No
  3. Add action: Send SMS
  4. Message: "Hi {first_name}, just checking in! I wanted to make sure you had everything you need. Do you have any questions I can help with?"

Step 4: Follow-up 2 (3 days later, if still no appointment)

  1. Add action: Wait → 3 days
  2. Add condition: "Has appointment booked?" → No
  3. Add action: Send SMS

Lesson 2: The Appointment Reminder System

No-shows and last-minute cancellations cost local businesses thousands per month. The average no-show rate for service businesses is 20-30%.

An automated reminder system drops that to under 5%.

Building Your Reminder Sequence

Step 1: Create the workflow

  1. Go to Automation → Workflows → Create New
  2. Name it "Appointment Reminders"
  3. Trigger: "Appointment booked"

Step 2: Confirmation (immediate)

  1. Send SMS: "You're all set, {first_name}! Your appointment with [Business Name] is confirmed for {appointment_date} at {appointment_time}. Reply CONFIRM to keep your spot or RESCHEDULE if you need to change it. See you then!"

Step 3: Reminder 1 (48 hours before)

  1. Wait until 48 hours before appointment
  2. Send SMS: "Reminder: You have an appointment with [Business Name] tomorrow at {appointment_time}. We're looking forward to seeing you! Reply CANCEL if you need to reschedule."

Step 4: Reminder 2 (2 hours before)

  1. Wait until 2 hours before appointment
  2. Send SMS: "See you in 2 hours, {first_name}! Our address is [address]. If you need to reach us, call [phone]."

Step 5: Post-appointment follow-up (1 hour after)

  1. Wait until 1 hour after appointment end time
  2. Send SMS: "Thanks for coming in today, {first_name}! We hope everything went great. If you have any questions, don't hesitate to reach out."
  3. Wait 1 hour
  4. Trigger review request (see Lesson 3)

Pro Tips

  • Allow easy cancellation. "Reply CANCEL" seems like it would increase cancellations, but it actually reduces no-shows. People who would have ghosted you instead cancel, which opens that slot for someone else.
  • Include the address. People forget where you are. Sounds obvious, but a surprising number of no-shows are because people couldn't find the location.
  • Personalize when possible. If you know what service they're booked for, include it: "Your [deep cleaning] appointment is tomorrow."

Lesson 1: Your Google Business Profile (The #1 Priority)

If you do ONE thing from this entire course, do this.

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the listing that shows up when someone searches for your type of business in your area. It's the box on the right side of Google with your hours, reviews, photos, and a call button.

For local businesses, this single listing drives more customers than your website, your social media, and your ads combined.

Setting Up Your Profile (If You Haven't)

  1. Go to business.google.com
  2. Click "Manage now"
  3. Enter your business name
  4. Choose your category (be specific — "Plumber" not "Home Services")
  5. Add your address (or service area if you go to customers)
  6. Add your phone number and website
  7. Verify your business (Google will send a postcard, call, or email)

Optimizing Your Profile (Do All of These)

Business Name

  • Use your exact real-world business name
  • Don't stuff keywords ("Joe's Plumbing Best Plumber in Dallas Plumbing Services") — Google penalizes this

Categories

  • Choose the MOST specific primary category
  • Add secondary categories for other services
  • Example: Primary: "Plumber" → Secondary: "Water Heater Installation Service", "Drain Cleaning Service"

Description

  • 750 characters max
  • Include: what you do, who you serve, what makes you different, your area
  • Example: "Family-owned plumbing company serving the Dallas area since 2008. We specialize in water heater installation, drain cleaning, and emergency repairs. Available 24/7 for emergencies. Licensed, insured, and backed by 200+ five-star reviews."

Photos

Services/Products

  • This helps you show up for specific searches

Hours

  • Keep them accurate and updated
  • Add special hours for holidays
  • If you offer 24/7 emergency service, list that

Q&A

  • Pre-load common questions and answers
