## Lesson 1: Your Google Business Profile (The #1 Priority)

If you do ONE thing from this entire course, do this.

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the listing that shows up when someone searches for your type of business in your area. It's the box on the right side of Google with your hours, reviews, photos, and a call button.

**For local businesses, this single listing drives more customers than your website, your social media, and your ads combined.**

### Setting Up Your Profile (If You Haven't)

1. Go to [business.google.com](https://business.google.com)
2. Click "Manage now"
3. Enter your business name
4. Choose your category (be specific — "Plumber" not "Home Services")
5. Add your address (or service area if you go to customers)
6. Add your phone number and website
7. Verify your business (Google will send a postcard, call, or email)

### Optimizing Your Profile (Do All of These)

**Business Name**
- Use your exact real-world business name
- Don't stuff keywords ("Joe's Plumbing Best Plumber in Dallas Plumbing Services") — Google penalizes this

**Categories**
- Choose the MOST specific primary category
- Add secondary categories for other services
- Example: Primary: "Plumber" → Secondary: "Water Heater Installation Service", "Drain Cleaning Service"

**Description**
- 750 characters max
- Include: what you do, who you serve, what makes you different, your area
- Example: "Family-owned plumbing company serving the Dallas area since 2008. We specialize in water heater installation, drain cleaning, and emergency repairs. Available 24/7 for emergencies. Licensed, insured, and backed by 200+ five-star reviews."

**Photos**
- Upload at least 10 photos minimum
- Include: storefront/vehicles, team photos, before/after work, equipment
- Add new photos monthly — businesses with 100+ photos get 520% more calls

**Services/Products**
- Add every service you offer with a brief description and price range
- This helps you show up for specific searches

**Hours**
- Keep them accurate and updated
- Add special hours for holidays
- If you offer 24/7 emergency service, list that

**Q&A**
- Pre-load common questions and answers
- "Do you offer free estimates?" → "Yes, we offer free estimates on all projects over $500."
- "What areas do you serve?" → "We serve [list your cities/areas]."
- Check regularly for new questions from the public

### The Posting Strategy

Google lets you post updates directly to your Business Profile — like social media posts, but they show up in Google search results.

**Post at least once per week:**

| Post Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Offer | "20% off all AC tune-ups this month. Call to schedule!" |
| Update | "Just completed a full kitchen remodel in [neighborhood]. Check out the before and after!" |
| Event | "Join us at the [City] Home Show this Saturday — Booth #42" |
| Product/Service | "Now offering tankless water heater installations. Learn more." |

**Each post should include:**
- A photo or short video
- Clear text (150-300 words)
- A call to action (Call now, Book online, Learn more)
- Your relevant service keywords (naturally, not stuffed)

## Lesson 2: Showing Up in Local Search (SEO Basics)

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) sounds complicated. For local businesses, it comes down to a few key things.

### How Google Decides Who Shows Up

When someone searches "plumber near me" or "dentist in Dallas," Google looks at three things:

| Factor | What It Means | How Important |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| **Relevance** | Does your business match what they're searching for? | Very important |
| **Distance** | How close are you to the searcher? | Very important |
| **Prominence** | How well-known and trusted is your business? | Important |

You can't control where you're located. But you CAN control relevance (your categories, services, content) and prominence (your reviews, links, citations).

### The 5 Things That Actually Move the Needle

**1. Get More Reviews (and Respond to All of Them)**

You already set up the review automation in Module 3. Here's the additional step:

Respond to every single review — positive or negative. Within 48 hours.

For positive reviews:
> "Thank you so much, [Name]! We're glad we could help with your [service]. We appreciate you trusting us with your [home/business]!"

For negative reviews (as covered in Module 3):
> "We're sorry about your experience, [Name]. We'd like to make this right — please call us at [phone] so we can discuss this directly."

Google has confirmed that responding to reviews improves your ranking.

**2. Keep Your Information Consistent Everywhere**

Your business Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP) should be identical across:
- Your website
- Google Business Profile
- Facebook
- Yelp
- Angi (formerly Angie's List)
- Nextdoor
- Your local chamber of commerce website
- Any other directory you're listed in

If your address says "123 Main Street" on Google but "123 Main St." on Yelp, that inconsistency hurts your ranking. Pick one format and use it everywhere.

**3. Build Local Citations**

A citation is any place online that mentions your business name, address, and phone number. The more quality citations you have, the more Google trusts that you're a real, established business.

**Where to get listed (all free):**

| Directory | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Yelp | Claim/create your listing |
| Facebook Business | Create or claim your page |
| Bing Places | Claim your listing |
| Apple Maps | Claim through Apple Business Connect |
| Nextdoor | Create a business page |
| Angi | Claim your listing |
| BBB | Get listed (free basic listing) |
| Local Chamber of Commerce | Join and get a website listing |
| Industry-specific directories | (e.g., Healthgrades for doctors, Houzz for contractors) |

Set aside one afternoon to claim all of these. Use the exact same NAP information every time.

**4. Add Location Content to Your Website**

If your website doesn't mention where you serve, Google won't know.

Add pages or sections for each area you serve:

> "Serving Dallas, Fort Worth, Plano, Frisco, McKinney, Allen, and surrounding areas."

Better yet, create a dedicated page for each major city:
- /dallas-plumber
- /fort-worth-plumber
- /plano-plumber

Each page should have:
- The city name in the title and headings
- A brief description of services in that area
- Any specific information (permits, common issues in that area)
- Your contact information

**5. Make Sure Your Website Is Fast and Mobile-Friendly**

60%+ of local searches happen on phones. If your website is slow or hard to use on a phone, you're losing customers.

Quick check:
1. Go to your website on your phone right now
2. Can you read everything without zooming?
3. Can you tap the phone number to call?
4. Does it load in under 3 seconds?
5. Can you easily find the contact information?

If any answer is "no," that's your first website fix.

## Lesson 3: Getting Found on Social Media

Social media for local businesses isn't about going viral. It's about being the obvious choice when someone in your area needs what you offer.

### The Platform Priority

| Platform | Priority | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| **Google Business Profile** | #1 | People actively searching for your service |
| **Facebook** | #2 | Still the largest platform for 30-65 age group. Community groups are gold. |
| **Instagram** | #3 | Great for visual businesses (landscaping, auto, beauty, restaurants) |
| **Nextdoor** | #4 | Hyperlocal — neighbors recommending businesses to neighbors |
| **TikTok** | #5 | Younger audience, great for reach, but less direct conversion for most local businesses |
| **LinkedIn** | #6 | B2B only (if you serve other businesses) |

**Start with #1 and #2. Add the others as you get comfortable.**

### The 80/20 Rule of Content

| Percentage | Content Type | Purpose |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| 80% | Helpful, interesting, entertaining | Build trust and following |
| 20% | Promotional (offers, CTAs) | Drive action |

If every post is "Buy my stuff," people stop following. If most posts are helpful and occasionally you mention an offer, people pay attention.

### Facebook Groups: Your Secret Weapon

Join every local Facebook group in your area:
- "[City] Community Group"
- "[City] Recommendations"
- "[Neighborhood] Nextdoor"
- "[City] Buy/Sell/Trade"
- Local parent groups, homeowner groups, etc.

**Don't spam.** Instead:
1. Answer questions when people ask for recommendations
2. Share helpful tips when relevant
3. Be a helpful community member first
4. When someone asks "Know a good [your service]?" — your name comes up naturally

**How to get recommended:** Do great work, ask for reviews, and be visible in the community. The recommendations flow naturally from there.

### Hashtag Strategy (Keep It Simple)

For Instagram and TikTok:
- 3-5 local hashtags: #[City]Business #[City]Life #[Neighborhood]
- 3-5 industry hashtags: #PlumbingTips #HomeImprovement #[YourTrade]
- 1-2 broader hashtags: #SmallBusiness #LocalBusiness

That's it. You don't need 30 hashtags. You need the right 8-10.

## Lesson 4: Simple Website Fixes That Make a Big Difference

You don't need a perfect website. You need one that converts visitors into callers/bookers.

### The 5-Second Test

Pull up your website. Look at it for 5 seconds. Can you immediately answer:

1. **What does this business do?**
2. **Where are they located?**
3. **How do I contact them?**
4. **Why should I choose them?**

If any answer is unclear, that's a problem.

### Must-Haves on Your Homepage

| Element | Why |
|---------|-----|
| Clear headline stating what you do | "Trusted Plumbing Services in Dallas" — not "Welcome to Our Website" |
| Phone number (clickable on mobile) | Make it stupid easy to call you |
| Service list or menu | People need to know you do what they need |
| Social proof (reviews, ratings, trust badges) | Builds instant credibility |
| Clear call to action | "Call Now" or "Book Online" — tell them what to do next |
| Your service area | "Serving Dallas, Fort Worth, and surrounding areas" |

### Click-to-Call Setup

On your website, make sure every phone number is a clickable link:

```html
<a href="tel:+15551234567">(555) 123-4567</a>
```

This seems obvious, but a shocking number of local business websites have their phone number as plain text that you can't tap to call on mobile.

### Add a Booking/Contact Form

Not everyone wants to call. Give them a form:
- Name
- Phone
- Email
- What service do you need?
- When are you available?

Connect this form to your GoHighLevel automation so submissions trigger your lead response workflow automatically.
